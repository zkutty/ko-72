Micro-season 37 of 72 · Jul 7 – Jul 11

Warm winds arrive.

Atsukaze itaru

The first truly hot winds sweep across Japan, carrying the dense humidity that signals high summer has begun.

Step outside and the air itself has weight — warm currents press against the skin like breath. The wind that arrives now is not cooling; it carries the accumulated heat of sun-baked earth and asphalt, the green scent of rice paddies reaching for the sky. Cicadas have not yet reached their crescendo, but in the stillness between gusts, you can hear the first tentative calls testing the summer air.

Nature notes

Rice plants stand knee-high in flooded paddies, their green now deepening toward a lustrous jade. Fireflies complete their brief season in mountain valleys while dragonflies — red-bodied akatombo still weeks away — patrol the edges of ponds. The sky takes on that particular summer haze, blue but never quite clear, the horizon always softened by moisture rising from the land.

Cultural note

Early July marks the beginning of Obon preparations in many regions, as families begin cleaning ancestral graves and purchasing supplies for the mid-August homecoming of spirits. In areas following the old lunar calendar, Tanabata decorations still flutter from bamboo branches outside homes and shopping arcades, the paper wishes — tanzaku — now faded by sun and occasional rain. This liminal period between festivals invites quiet anticipation.
